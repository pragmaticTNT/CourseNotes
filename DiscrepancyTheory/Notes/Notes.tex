\lecture{13}{Modules and Vector Spaces}{Shoup}{Lily Li}

\section{Definitions}
Let $R$ be a ring (i.e. commutative ring with unity).

\begin{definition}
An \textbf{R-module} is a set $M$ together with an addition operation on $M$ and a function $\mu: R \times M \leftarrow M$, such that $M$ under addition forms an abelian group and for all $c,d \in R$ and $\alpha, \beta \in M$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[Scalar Mult:] $\mu (c, \mu(d,\alpha)) = \mu(cd, \alpha)$;
\item[Set Distrib:] $\mu(c + d, \alpha) = \mu(c, \alpha) + \mu(d, \alpha)$;
\item[Scalar Distrib:] $\mu(c, \alpha + \beta) = \mu(c, \alpha) + \mu(c, \beta)$;
\item[Scalar Id:] $\mu(1_R, \alpha) = \alpha$.
\end{enumerate}
You should think of these as more general vector spaces ($R$ need not be a field here).
\end{definition}
